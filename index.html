About Me

I am a graduate student at the University of New Haven pursuing a Master's in Electrical Engineering with a focus on robust coordination in heterogeneous communicative Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL). My research centers on developing power regularization mechanisms to enhance robustness in agent communication.

With a background in Power Electronics from Isfahan University of Technology, I've transitioned to the exciting field of AI and MARL, where I work on creating resilient communication protocols for multi-agent systems.

Research

Robust Coordination in Heterogeneous Communicative MARL

My current research focuses on developing novel power regularization mechanisms for robust communication in MARL. This work addresses the challenges of adversarial communication and misalignment in cooperative multi-agent systems.

University of New Haven, SAIL Lab (Supervisor: Dr. Vahid Behzadan)

High Step-up DC-DC Converters

Designed and implemented a novel high step-up DC-DC converter with reduced switching and conduction losses for renewable energy applications.

Isfahan University of Technology (Supervisor: Dr. Ehsan Adib)

Projects

Communicative Power Regularization in MARL

Developed and implemented Communicative Power Regularization (CPR), a novel method to defend against adversarial communication by penalizing agents' over-reliance on team communication in the Grid Coverage environment.

MalConv-based Malware Classifier

Developed a real-time malware detection model using a MalConv-based architecture trained on the EMBER dataset, and deployed it with a Streamlit interface integrated with AWS SageMaker for cloud-based inference on raw .exe files.

MARL Grid-World Simulation Environment
