My Journey

From Circuit Boards to Neural Networks

My academic journey began in the world of Power Electronics at Isfahan University of Technology, where I developed a fascination with circuit design and energy systems. I spent countless hours in labs designing DC-DC converters and optimizing power systems.

But as they say, life is all about evolution. In 2024, I found myself increasingly drawn to the exciting field of Artificial Intelligence. What started as curiosity quickly turned into passion when I discovered Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL).

Now at the University of New Haven, my research focuses on making multi-agent AI systems secure and trustworthy. I develop novel frameworks to characterize, detect, and mitigate a wide range of deceptive behaviors, from adversarial communication to sophisticated strategic manipulation. It's a thrilling transition that combines my engineering mindset with cutting-edge AI research.

Current Research

Deception and Deception Mitigation in Multi-Agent Systems

At the SAIL Lab (University of New Haven), I work with Dr. Vahid Behzadan on the critical challenge of ensuring AI systems are safe from deceptive manipulation. My research focuses on developing a framework for understanding and mitigating deception in Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL).

Our recently proposed method, Communicative Power Regularization (CPR), enables cooperative agents to maintain effective coordination even under adversarial or misaligned communication. This work has been accepted for publication at ECAI 2025 and selected for poster presentation at CoCoMARL 2025 and NYRL 2025. I also presented it at Hartford AI Day 2025, where it received insightful feedback from the AI research community.
